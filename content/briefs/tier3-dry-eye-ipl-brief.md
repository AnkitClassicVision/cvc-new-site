## Research Summary

### What is IPL for Dry Eye?
- Non-laser high-intensity light therapy
- FDA-approved for dry eye disease due to MGD
- Treats skin near eyelids with controlled light pulses
- Wavelengths 500-1200 nm
- Lumenis offers first FDA-approved IPL for dry eye

### How It Works
- Thermal effect liquefies and softens meibum (oils)
- Coagulates abnormal blood vessels (telangiectasia)
- Reduces inflammation and pro-inflammatory markers
- Decreases bacteria at eyelid margin
- Often combined with meibomian gland expression (MGX)

### Treatment Protocol
- 4 sessions spaced 2-4 weeks apart
- Each session: 10-15 minutes
- No downtime
- Improvement often noticed after 1st or 2nd treatment
- Monthly maintenance may be needed

### Efficacy (Per Mayo Clinic & Clinical Studies)
- Effective and safe for severe dry eye
- Improves symptoms and objective indicators
- Better tear break-up time
- Improved lipid layer thickness
- Better Schirmer test results
- Effects may diminish after 9 months without maintenance

### Safety
- Non-invasive, well-accepted by patients
- Rare major adverse reactions
- Some temporary redness possible
