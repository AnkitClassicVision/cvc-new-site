- Why it's getting worse worldwide
- The problem with "just getting stronger glasses"

  - Retinal detachment: 5-6x higher risk
  - Glaucoma: 2-3x higher risk
  - Myopic macular degeneration
  - Early cataracts
- The earlier it starts, the worse it gets

### Section 2: What is Myopia Control?
**H2**: "Myopia Control: Slowing Down Nearsightedness"
- Myopia control vs. myopia correction
- How it works: Signal the eye to slow growth
- Studies show 50-60% reduction in progression
- Best started between ages 8-12 (earlier better)
- Not about avoiding glasses; about protecting long-term eye health

### Section 3: Treatment Options
**H2**: "Your Child's Myopia Control Options"

#### MiSight Daily Contact Lenses
- What: FDA-approved daily disposable lenses
- How: Special optics signal eye to slow elongation
- For whom: Children 8-12 years, able to handle contacts
- Results: Average 59% slower myopia progression
- Benefits: Easy, daily disposable, no overnight wear
- Link: misight-lenses-for-myopia-control.html

#### Orthokeratology (Ortho-K)
- What: Specially designed lenses worn overnight
- How: Gently reshape cornea while sleeping
- For whom: Active children, swimmers, athletes
- Results: Clear vision during day without glasses/contacts
- Benefits: No daytime correction needed
- Link: ortho-k-lenses-for-myopia-control.html

#### Low-Dose Atropine Eye Drops
- What: Diluted atropine drops used daily
- How: Slows eye growth through muscle relaxation
- For whom: Combined with other treatments or younger children
- Results: 50-80% slower progression depending on dose
- Benefits: Easy to administer, no contact lens handling
- Link: myopia-control-atropine-eye-drops.html

#### Multifocal Soft Contact Lenses
- What: Specialized multifocal lenses
- How: Creates peripheral defocus
- For whom: Those preferring soft lenses over Ortho-K
- Results: 25-50% slower progression
- Link: myopia-control-multifocal-lenses.html
