### Section 2: What to Expect During Your Exam
**H2**: "What Happens During Your Comprehensive Eye Exam"
**Content Elements** (process walkthrough):
1. **Medical History Review** - Understanding your overall health
2. **Visual Acuity Testing** - The classic "which is clearer" test
3. **Refraction Assessment** - Determining your exact prescription
4. **Eye Muscle Function** - How your eyes work together
5. **Peripheral Vision Testing** - Checking side vision
6. **Pupil Response** - Evaluating neurological function
7. **Intraocular Pressure** - Glaucoma screening
8. **Anterior Eye Examination** - Cornea, iris, lens health
9. **Retinal Examination** - Back of the eye evaluation
10. **Discussion & Recommendations** - We explain everything clearly

### Section 3: Our Diagnostic Technology
**H2**: "Advanced Technology for Accurate Diagnosis"
- **Digital Retinal Imaging**: See your eye health in high definition
- **Optical Coherence Tomography (OCT)**: Cross-section imaging of your retina
- **Visual Field Testing**: Peripheral vision assessment
- **Corneal Topography**: Detailed corneal mapping
- Why technology matters for early detection

### Section 4: Conditions We Detect
**H2**: "Eye Exams Save Vision: Conditions We Detect Early"
- Glaucoma (the "silent thief of sight")
- Macular Degeneration
- Diabetic Retinopathy
- Cataracts
- Dry Eye Disease
- High blood pressure signs
- Early diabetes indicators

### Section 5: Who Needs an Eye Exam
**H2**: "How Often Should You Have an Eye Exam?"
- Children: Starting at age 3, then before school, annually
- Adults 18-60: Every 1-2 years
- Adults 60+: Annually
- Diabetics: Annually (or more frequently)
- Contact lens wearers: Annually
- Those with family history of eye disease: Annually

### Section 6: Preparing for Your Exam
**H2**: "How to Prepare for Your Eye Exam"
- Bring current glasses and contacts
- Know your family eye health history
- List any medications you take
- Note any vision concerns or questions
- Plan for dilated exam (bring sunglasses)
