Computer Eye Strain

Care for Computer Eye Strain

The rise of digital devices has brought convenience and connectivity to daily life, but it has also introduced a new set of challenges for eye health. Computer eye strain, also known as digital eye strain, is a growing issue. At Classic Vision Care, we combine decades of expertise with a patient-first approach to help alleviate your symptoms and ensure your eyes stay healthy in our ever-connected world.

What Is Computer Eye Strain?

Computer eye strain refers to a range of eye and vision problems resulting from prolonged use of digital devices like computers, tablets, and smartphones. This condition is increasingly prevalent in today’s digital era, where hours of screen time are part of daily routines at work, school, and home.

The strain occurs because the eyes have to work harder when viewing digital screens compared to printed materials. Factors such as screen glare, poor lighting, improper viewing distances, and uncorrected vision problems can exacerbate the issue.

What Are The Symptoms Of Computer Eye Strain? 

Eye strain from computer screens manifests in various ways, and the symptoms can differ from person to person. Common signs include:

  • Eye discomfort and fatigue: Your eyes may feel tired or strained after screen use.
  • Blurred vision: Difficulty focusing on the screen or transitioning between distances.
  • Dry eyes: Reduced blinking can lead to dryness, redness, and irritation.
  • Headaches: Persistent screen use often causes tension headaches.
  • Neck and shoulder pain: Poor posture while using devices can result in muscular discomfort.

If you’re experiencing any of these symptoms, visiting Classic Vision Care for an evaluation can help identify the cause and provide tailored solutions.

What Treatment Is Available For Computer Eye Strain?

At Classic Vision Care, we offer a range of treatments designed to alleviate eye strain from computers. Our skilled eye doctors provide personalized care to ensure the best results. Some common treatments include:

Specialized lenses designed to reduce screen glare, filter blue light, and optimize focus for digital screens.

Prescription updates or computer glasses when needed to make screen distance more comfortable.

Artificial tears to help alleviate dryness and maintain moisture.

Guidance on workspace adjustments and ergonomic improvements, such as proper lighting and screen positioning.

How To Prevent Computer Eye Strain

Preventing computer monitor eye strain is just as important as treating it. Here are some practical steps you can take:

  • Follow the 20-20-20 rule: Every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds to reduce eye fatigue.
  • Adjust your screen settings: Optimize brightness, contrast, and font size for comfort.
  • Maintain proper posture: Sit with your back straight, and keep your screen at eye level.
  • Take breaks: Schedule regular breaks to rest your eyes and stretch your body.
  • Use blue light filters: Consider screen protectors or glasses with blue light blocking technology.

Our Other Eye Care Services

Classic Vision Care has a range of services to help with the care of your eyes.

Diabetic eye care – it is vitally important, if you have diabetes, to have your eyes checked regularly. Unchecked, diabetes can have a significant impact on your eyes and eyesight.

Myopia – commonly referred to as nearsightedness, this is a condition that develops early in a person’s life. The sooner you diagnose, the sooner we can help clear your vision and have you reading the world.

Macular Degeneration – this serious condition affects older people, where the macula, a key part of your retina, can be damaged, causing central vision blindness. Often, you don’t know you have this condition until it has already caused irreversible damage, so early detection is the best solution.
