What to Expect

What Happens During a Comprehensive Eye Exam

Every exam is personalized, but most visits follow a clear flow. We’ll guide you through each step and explain what we’re learning about your eyes along the way.

Your exam, step by step

  1. 1

    Medical and vision history

    We review your health, medications, and any symptoms, including headaches, dryness, or eye strain.

  2. 2

    Visual acuity testing

    The classic “letters on the chart” test gives a baseline for how well you see at distance and near.

  3. 3

    Refraction and prescription check

    We fine-tune your prescription so your glasses or contacts feel clear and comfortable.

  4. 4

    Eye teaming and focusing

    We check how your eyes work together, which matters for reading, screens, and headaches.

  5. 5

    Eye pressure and glaucoma screening

    We measure eye pressure and evaluate the optic nerve to watch for glaucoma risk.

  6. 6

    Front-of-eye evaluation

    We examine the cornea, lens, and eyelids. This is also where dry eye signs often show up.

  7. 7

    Retina and optic nerve evaluation

    We look at the back of the eye and document your eye health using imaging when appropriate.

  8. 8

    Peripheral vision testing (when needed)

    Visual field testing helps detect blind spots and monitor conditions like glaucoma.

  9. 9

    Dry eye assessment (if symptoms are present)

    If your eyes burn, water, or feel gritty, we can discuss options through our Dry Eye Spa.

  10. 10

    Review and recommendations

    We summarize what we found and help you choose next steps that fit your needs and your budget.

How long does it take?

Most comprehensive eye exams take about 45-60 minutes, depending on your history, symptoms, and the testing you need. If you’re updating contacts or discussing dry eye treatment, your visit may take longer.

If you have time constraints, tell us when you schedule. We’ll help you book the right appointment type.

Prevention is Key

Why Regular Eye Exams Matter

Many serious eye conditions, including glaucoma, macular degeneration, and diabetic retinopathy, develop silently with no early symptoms. By the time you notice vision changes, permanent damage may have already occurred.

Regular comprehensive eye exams allow us to detect these conditions in their earliest stages, when treatment is most effective. We can also identify signs of systemic health issues like diabetes and high blood pressure before you experience other symptoms.

Most adults benefit from an eye exam every 1-2 years. Children, seniors, contact lens wearers, and patients with risk factors often need yearly care. We’ll recommend a schedule based on your eyes and your health history.

Recommended Exam Frequency

Children (6-17) Annually
Adults (18-64) Every 1-2 years
Seniors (65+) Annually
Diabetics Annually or more
Contact lens wearers Annually

FAQ

Comprehensive Eye Exam FAQs

Still deciding? Here are quick answers to the questions we hear most often from patients in Kennesaw and Marietta.

How long does a comprehensive eye exam take?

Plan for about 45-60 minutes. If you’re updating contact lenses or discussing dry eye treatment, your visit may take longer.

Will my eyes be dilated?

Dilation is recommended for many patients to fully evaluate retinal health. If it’s appropriate for you, we’ll explain why and what to expect.

Can I drive after my exam?

Most patients can drive, but dilation may cause temporary light sensitivity and blur. If you’re concerned, bring a driver.

Do you accept vision insurance?

We work with many plans. Check our insurance page or call us and we’ll help verify coverage.

What if I have dry, irritated eyes?

Tell us during your exam. We can evaluate symptoms and, when needed, guide you to treatment options through our Dry Eye Spa and advanced therapies.

Common questions

Clear answers help you move forward with confidence.

How often should I schedule Comprehensive Eye Exams That Go Beyond 20/20?

Many patients come in yearly, but the right schedule depends on age, prescriptions, and health history. We will recommend the interval that matches your risk factors and goals.

Will you check more than just my prescription?

Yes. We look at eye health as well as vision. That can include screening for glaucoma and macular degeneration, along with other findings that can affect comfort and clarity.

What if I have dry eye or headaches?

Tell us. Comfort symptoms help guide testing and lens choices. A prescription update alone may not be enough if dryness or eye strain is part of the picture.
