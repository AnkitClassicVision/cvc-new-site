Conditions We Treat

Finding Relief Starts Here

Meibomian Gland Dysfunction

Blocked oil glands that prevent proper tear film. Our thermal treatments restore healthy gland function.

Blepharitis

Inflammation of the eyelids causing irritation and crusting. BlephEx provides deep, effective cleaning.

Evaporative Dry Eye

Tears evaporate too quickly due to oil layer deficiency. IPL and MiBoFlo restore proper tear composition.

Aqueous Deficient Dry Eye

Not enough tears being produced. Punctal plugs and prescription drops help retain and supplement moisture.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

What causes dry eye syndrome?

Dry eye can be caused by many factors including aging, hormonal changes, medications, environmental conditions, prolonged screen use, and underlying health conditions. The two main types are evaporative dry eye (caused by blocked oil glands) and aqueous deficient dry eye (insufficient tear production). Our comprehensive evaluation will identify your specific cause so we can recommend the most effective treatment.

How many treatments will I need?

Treatment frequency varies based on the severity of your condition and the specific treatments recommended. MiBoFlo and IPL typically involve a series of 3-4 initial treatments spaced 2-4 weeks apart, followed by maintenance treatments every 6-12 months. BlephEx may be done quarterly. During your consultation, we will create a personalized treatment plan tailored to your needs.

Are the treatments painful?

Our treatments are designed with your comfort in mind. MiBoFlo feels like a gentle, warm massage and most patients find it quite relaxing. IPL may cause mild warmth or a slight snapping sensation. BlephEx involves a gentle cleaning sensation along your lash line. Punctal plugs are inserted quickly with minimal discomfort. Our spa-like environment and careful technique ensure the most comfortable experience possible.

Does insurance cover dry eye treatment?

Coverage varies by insurance plan and treatment type. The diagnostic evaluation and prescription eye drops are often covered by medical insurance. Treatments like IPL, MiBoFlo, and BlephEx may be considered elective by some insurers. We will provide you with detailed pricing and help you understand your coverage options during your consultation. We also offer flexible payment plans to make treatment accessible.

How long do the results last?

Results vary depending on the treatment and individual factors. After completing an initial IPL or MiBoFlo series, many patients enjoy 4-6 months of significant relief before maintenance treatments are needed. Punctal plugs can provide ongoing relief for months to years. Scleral lenses offer continuous relief while worn. We will work with you to develop a maintenance plan that keeps your eyes comfortable long-term.

What makes your Dry Eye Spa different from other clinics?

Unlike clinical-focused practices, we have created a spa-like experience that makes treatment something you actually look forward to. Our calming environment, unhurried appointments, and comprehensive treatment menu set us apart. We offer advanced treatments like MiBoFlo and scleral lenses that many practices do not have. Most importantly, we take the time to understand your specific condition and create a personalized treatment plan rather than a one-size-fits-all approach.

Details That Matter

A more helpful guide to Your Dry Eye Oasis

Dry eye is more than an occasional annoyance. If your eyes burn, feel gritty, or fluctuate between blurry and clear, it often means the tear film is not staying stable on the eye. Our goal is to identify what is driving your symptoms and build a plan you can realistically maintain.

Quick ways to get more value from your visit

  • Tell us when symptoms are worst, such as mornings, afternoons, or during screen time.
  • Bring any drops or supplements you have tried so we can build a clearer plan together.
  • If you wear contacts, share your wear schedule and comfort level so we can tailor treatment.

Common questions

Clear answers help you move forward with confidence.

Is dry eye always a chronic problem?

Dry eye can be ongoing, but symptoms can improve significantly with the right plan. We focus on identifying the drivers, then combining in-office options with sustainable home habits.

Do I have to stop wearing contacts?

Not always. Some people can keep wearing contacts once dryness is better controlled. We will talk about comfortable options and a schedule that protects your eyes.
