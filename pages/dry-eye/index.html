Root Cause

Why Your Eyes Feel Dry (And Why Drops Aren’t Enough)

“Dry eye” can be confusing because it doesn’t always feel dry. Your eyes may burn, water, feel gritty, or look red. Many people try drop after drop and still feel irritated by mid-day.

One reason is that dry eye often isn’t just a lack of tears. The tear film is a blend of water, oils, and mucus. If one layer is off, tears can evaporate too quickly and your eyes can stay inflamed.

Dry eye usually fits into one of two patterns. Some people don’t make enough watery tears. Others make tears, but the protective oil layer doesn’t spread well, so tears evaporate faster than they should. Many patients have a mix of both. That’s why the “best” drop for one person can do almost nothing for someone else.

Our Dry Eye Spa focuses on identifying what’s driving your symptoms, then building a plan that fits your daily life. If you want the full treatment list, visit our Dry Eye Treatment Menu.

Common reasons dry eye lingers

  • Oil gland dysfunction (meibomian glands) that causes tears to evaporate faster
  • Inflammation along the eyelids or ocular surface
  • Screen time and reduced blinking
  • Contacts that no longer fit your eyes and tear film
  • Environment (heat/AC, low humidity, fans) and certain medications

Relief depends on the cause. We’ll recommend treatment after a complete evaluation.

FAQ

Dry Eye Treatment FAQs

Quick answers to common questions from dry eye patients in Kennesaw, East Cobb, and the greater Atlanta area.

How many treatments will I need?

It depends on your dry eye type and severity. Some patients improve with a focused series of treatments, while others do best with a maintenance plan. We’ll outline expectations after your evaluation.

Is treatment painful?

Most in-office therapies are designed to be comfortable. Many patients describe MiBoFlo as warm and relaxing. If a step may cause discomfort, we’ll tell you beforehand.

Will insurance cover dry eye treatment?

Coverage varies by plan and by treatment type. Medical evaluation may be covered, while certain therapies can be out-of-pocket. Our team can help you understand options.

Can I wear contact lenses if I have dry eye?

Often, yes. The key is improving the tear film and choosing a contact lens strategy that fits your eyes. For severe dry eye, scleral lenses can be a strong option.

How long do results last?

Dry eye is often a chronic condition that can flare with seasons, hormones, and screen time. Our goal is long-term comfort. Some patients do well with periodic maintenance, and many feel best when they keep up with a simple home routine.

Details That Matter

A more helpful guide to Spa-Like Relief for Dry Eyes

Dry eye is more than an occasional annoyance. If your eyes burn, feel gritty, or fluctuate between blurry and clear, it often means the tear film is not staying stable on the eye. Our goal is to identify what is driving your symptoms and build a plan you can realistically maintain.

For many patients, the fastest improvement comes from pairing the right in-office option with consistent at-home care. We talk through what you have tried, what helped, and what did not. Then we design a step-by-step approach that fits your comfort goals and your schedule.

Common questions

Clear answers help you move forward with confidence.

Is dry eye always a chronic problem?

Dry eye can be ongoing, but symptoms can improve significantly with the right plan. We focus on identifying the drivers, then combining in-office options with sustainable home habits.

Do I have to stop wearing contacts?

Not always. Some people can keep wearing contacts once dryness is better controlled. We will talk about comfortable options and a schedule that protects your eyes.

How do you diagnose the cause?

We evaluate the tear film and eyelids and ask detailed questions about symptoms and triggers. This helps us choose treatments that match what is actually driving your dryness.

How soon might I feel relief?

Some people notice changes quickly, while others improve over several weeks. We will set expectations and adjust your plan based on how your eyes respond.

Can I combine treatments?

Yes. Dry eye is often multi-layered. A thoughtful combination can be more effective than relying on a single option alone.

Do warm compresses help?

They can, especially when oil gland function is part of the issue. We can show you a safe approach and suggest a routine that fits your day without feeling overwhelming.
